About Us

At Child Labour and Food Management Company, we are dedicated to ensuring that children around the world are given the chance to reach their full potential. We work to eliminate child labor and provide access to quality food and nutrition for those in need. Our mission is to make a difference in the lives of children and their families by providing food security and support for those affected by poverty.

Our team is comprised of experts in the fields of child labor, nutrition, and food management. We strive to create partnerships with leading organizations and experts in order to create innovative approaches to tackling child labor and hunger.

We are committed to making a difference in the lives of those affected by child labor and hunger. We are dedicated to creating a world where every child can have a chance to live a life free from exploitation and poverty.

How We Work

  • Advocate for the implementation of laws and regulations that protect the rights of children and guarantee access to basic services, such as education and healthcare.
  • Develop and implement public awareness campaigns in order to educate people about the inherent harm of child labor.
  • Work with governments and local organizations to create opportunities for children to gain access to education and skills training programs.
  • Engage with employers, trade unions and other organizations to ensure that all workers are given a fair wage and decent working conditions.
  • Provide resources to help families and communities understand their rights and responsibilities to protect children from exploitation.
  • Design and implement programs to support families in finding alternatives to child labor.
  • Strengthen labor market institutions and policies in order to reduce the need for child labor.
  • Support the development of sustainable and equitable food systems through capacity building, research and awareness-raising activities.
  • Promote food security and nutrition through local initiatives that support small-scale farmers and producers.
  • Facilitate access to markets and resources for marginalized communities and small-scale producers.
